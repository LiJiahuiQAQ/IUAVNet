The First International Workshop on Intelligent UAVs for next-generation networks (IUAVNet)

in Conjunction with the 23rd International Conference on Algorithms and Architectures for Parallel Processing (ICA3PP 2023, CCF RANK C), Tianjin, China, 20-22 October 2023

Call for Papers

Unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) can be equipped with communication and decision capacities. In this case, coordination and cooperation of UAVs are promising technology to adopt and aggregate resources dispersed by multiple UAVs for next-generation communications and networks. By carefully designing the decision-making and coordinate schemes, intelligent UAVs can provide high throughput, low interference, physical layer security, and energy-efficient network services.

This workshop aims at bringing together engineers and researchers working on solutions to this and related research challenges to share their new ideas, latest findings, and state-of-the-art results. The interested topics include but not limited to:

  • Channel measurement and modeling for UAVs
  • Performance analysis of Intelligent UAV swarm communication systems
  • Intelligent distributed antenna design for UAVs.
  • Spectrum management and multiple access schemes for UAVs
  • Interference mitigation of UAVs or intelligent UAV swarm-based low interference communication
  • Cyber security and physical-layer security of intelligent UAVs
  • Artificial intelligence for enabling the autonomous decision of UAVs
  • Energy-efficient multi-UAV communications
  • Adaptive protocol and QoS for intelligent UAVs
  • Intelligent UAV experiment and prototyping
  • UAVs cooperate with IoTs
  • Edge computing enabled by UAV servers
  • UAV-assisted mobile edge computing systems
  • UAV-enabled space-air-ground integrated network
  • Big data in Intelligent UAVs
  • Intelligent UAV communications with other advanced technologies

Submitted manuscripts must be prepared according to IEEE Computer Society Proceedings Format (double column, 10pt font, letter paper) and submitted in PDF file format. The manuscript should be no longer than 6 pages and at least 4 pages. Submitted manuscripts must not contain previously published material or be under consideration for publication in another conference or journal.

Accepted papers will be included in the conference proceedings and published in the IEEE Xplore digital library (indexed by EI). At least one author of any accepted paper must register and present the paper at the conference. After registration, all of the accepted papers will be recommended to SCI journal SPECIAL ISSUE, such as COMNET, Sensors. We will plan it in the future.
